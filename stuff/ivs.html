Comprehensive Guide to French Tenses

Tense Meaning Usage Example Sentences Translation
Présent Current action or state
  • Describes actions happening now.
  • States general truths or habits.
  • Je mange une pomme.
  • Nous allons au cinéma.
  • Il fait beau aujourd'hui.
  • I am eating an apple.
  • We are going to the cinema.
  • It is nice today.
Imparfait Ongoing or habitual action in the past
  • Describes background situations in the past.
  • Indicates repeated actions or habits.
  • Sets the scene in storytelling.
  • Quand j'étais jeune, je jouais au parc.
  • Il faisait beau chaque jour.
  • Nous regardions la télévision.
  • When I was young, I played in the park.
  • It was nice every day.
  • We were watching television.
Passé Composé Completed action in the past
  • Describes specific completed actions at a certain time.
  • Indicates actions that have an effect on the present.
  • J'ai mangé une pomme.
  • Nous avons fini le projet.
  • Ils sont allés au cinéma.
  • I ate an apple.
  • We finished the project.
  • They went to the cinema.
Plus-que-parfait Action that had occurred before another past action
  • Describes actions that were completed before another past event.
  • Indicates the order of past events.
  • J'avais mangé avant de partir.
  • Elle était déjà partie quand je suis arrivé.
  • Nous avions vu ce film auparavant.
  • I had eaten before leaving.
  • She had already left when I arrived.
  • We had seen that movie before.
Passé Simple Completed action in a literary context
  • Used mainly in written French to narrate events in stories.
  • Describes actions that are completed in a formal context.
  • Il fut un grand roi.
  • Elle parla de ses aventures.
  • Nous vivions dans un château.
  • He was a great king.
  • She spoke of her adventures.
  • We lived in a castle.
Passé Antérieur Action that had occurred before another action in a literary context
  • Used in literature to describe an action that was completed before another past action.
  • Il eut mangé avant d'arriver.
  • Quand elle eut fini, elle sortit.
  • Nous eûmes terminé le travail.
  • He had eaten before arriving.
  • When she had finished, she went out.
  • We had finished the work.
Futur Simple Action that will occur
  • Describes actions that will happen in the future.
  • Je mangerai une pomme.
  • Nous irons à la plage.
  • Ils joueront au football.
  • I will eat an apple.
  • We will go to the beach.
  • They will play soccer.
Futur Antérieur Action that will have occurred before another future action
  • Describes an action that will be completed before another future event.
  • J'aurai mangé avant de partir.
  • Ils auront terminé le travail.
  • Elle sera arrivée avant moi.
  • I will have eaten before leaving.
  • They will have finished the work.
  • She will have arrived before me.
Conditionnel Présent Hypothetical action
  • Describes actions that would happen under certain conditions.
  • Je mangerais une pomme si j'avais faim.
  • Nous irions au cinéma si nous avions le temps.
  • Ils accepteraient l'invitation.
  • I would eat an apple if I were hungry.
  • We would go to the cinema if we had time.
  • They would accept the invitation.
Conditionnel Passé Action that would have occurred
  • Describes actions that would have happened under certain conditions in the past.
  • J'aurais mangé une pomme si j'avais eu faim.
  • Ils auraient assisté à la réunion s'ils avaient été invités.
  • Nous serions partis plus tôt.
  • I would have eaten an apple if I had been hungry.
  • They would have attended the meeting if they had been invited.
  • We would have left earlier.
Subjonctif Présent Desire, emotion, or uncertainty
  • Used to express wishes, emotions, doubts, or necessities.
  • Il faut que je mange une pomme.
  • Je souhaite qu'il vienne.
  • Il est important que nous soyons prêts.
  • It is necessary that I eat an apple.
  • I wish he would come.
  • It is important that we are ready.
Subjonctif Passé Desire, emotion, or uncertainty about a completed action
  • Used to express wishes or doubts about actions that have already been completed.
  • Je doute qu'il ait mangé une pomme.
  • Il est triste qu'elle ne soit pas venue.
  • Nous avons peur qu'ils n'aient pas compris.
  • I doubt that he ate an apple.
  • He is sad that she did not come.
  • We are afraid they did not understand.
Impératif Command or request
  • Used for giving orders or making requests.
  • Can also suggest or invite someone to do something.
  • Mange une pomme!
  • Fais tes devoirs!
  • Allons-y!
  • Eat an apple!
  • Do your homework!
  • Let's go!

Imparfait and Passé Simple in French

Imparfait (Imperfect)

Definition: The imparfait is used for actions that are ongoing, habitual, or descriptive in the past.

When to Use Imparfait:

  • Ongoing Actions: When something was happening over time.
    Example: Je lisais un livre. (I was reading a book.)
  • Habitual Actions: For actions that happened regularly in the past.
    Example: Chaque été, nous allions à la plage. (Every summer, we went to the beach.)
  • Descriptions: To describe the scene, setting, or feelings in the past.
    Example: Il faisait beau et les oiseaux chantaient. (It was nice out, and the birds were singing.)
  • Background Information: Providing context for other actions.
    Example: Quand j'étais enfant, j'aimais jouer dehors. (When I was a child, I loved playing outside.)

Everyday Usage:

You’ll hear the imparfait in everyday conversations, storytelling, or when recounting past experiences. It’s common in spoken French and informal writing.

Passé Simple (Simple Past)

Definition: The passé simple is used for actions that are completed in the past, often in a sequential or narrative form.

When to Use Passé Simple:

  • Completed Actions: To describe actions that happened and were finished at a specific time in the past.
    Example: Il entra dans la salle et parla au professeur. (He entered the room and spoke to the teacher.)
  • Narrative Events: It’s used to move the story forward in a more formal or dramatic way, especially in literature.
    Example: Elle se leva, regarda par la fenêtre, et soupira. (She stood up, looked out the window, and sighed.)
  • Formal Writing: You might find it in newspapers, historical accounts, or literature, such as novels or stories.

Everyday Usage:

In everyday conversation, you won’t often hear the passé simple. Instead, it’s primarily found in written texts like books, historical writings, and formal essays. It adds a sense of elegance or drama to the narrative.

Clarifying "Literary Context"

When I mentioned "literary context," I meant that the passé simple is more commonly used in written French, especially in stories, literature, and formal writing. For example, if you're reading a novel, the author might use the passé simple to describe a sequence of events in a story.

In contrast, the imparfait is used in both spoken and written contexts because it describes how things were, ongoing actions, or repeated events. You would use it to share memories, tell stories, or talk about past habits.

Key Takeaways

  • Imparfait: Think of it as the "backdrop" of a story, setting the scene, describing feelings, or detailing ongoing actions. You use it frequently in conversations.
  • Passé Simple: Think of it as the "action" of the story, used for specific completed events or actions, mostly in writing. It gives a formal tone to narratives.

Example Conversation

Let’s imagine a simple conversation about a childhood memory:

  • A: Qu'est-ce que tu faisais quand tu étais enfant? (What did you do when you were a child?)
  • B: Je jouais au football et je faisais du vélo. (I played soccer and rode my bike.) [Imparfait - ongoing/habitual actions]
  • A: Quel jour as-tu appris à faire du vélo? (What day did you learn to ride a bike?)
  • B: J'ai appris un jour d'été, et ma mère m'a aidé. (I learned one summer day, and my mom helped me.) [Passé Simple - completed action]

In this example, B uses the imparfait to describe regular activities from childhood, while the passé simple is used to indicate a specific event that occurred.
